What is TreesCount! 2015?

TreesCount! 2015 is NYC Parks’ effort to map and catalogue every street tree on every block in New York City. Register to become a voluntreer today!

Do I need to know a lot about trees to get involved?

No prior experience is required! We will help you learn everything you need to know to map street trees.

How do I volunteer?

Getting involved is easy! To get started, register online and take our brief virtual training course. Then RSVP and attend a field training event with NYC Parks or with a Census partner group to gain hands-on field knowledge on how to identify and map street trees.

At the training event, we’ll have staff teaching you how to apply what you’ve learned, answer any questions you have, supply necessary mapping tools and a Tree Species ID guide, and get you mapping right away with other newly trained volunteers! Start your online training here!

Where can I volunteer to map street trees?

We are hosting events in neighborhoods throughout all five boroughs of New York City. Many local organizations are contributing to the mapping effort, and you will be able to participate in events hosted by TreesCount! partner groups or by NYC Parks. After you’ve completed your online training, find training and mapping events near you here.

What is the difference between a training event and a mapping event?

Training events are designed to help you apply what you learned in the virtual training course, so you can master the TreeKIT mapping method and the Treecorder software before collecting data for TreesCount! 2015. The first hour of a training event is spent learning data collection tips and helpful mapping reminders. After your training is complete, you will map in teams for the duration of the event. Mapping events are intended for voluntreers who have already been to a training event in the field. No instruction period is included; voluntreers will start mapping right away!

Do I need to bring anything to a training event?

Just a love of trees and a willingness to learn! At the training event, you will receive a TreesCount! Gear Pack with a Tree Species ID guide and a measuring tape, along with a volunteer badge and lanyard, for you to keep and reuse throughout the duration of the census. NYC Parks and our partners will provide you with a measuring wheel and a tablet for mapping trees and collecting data. These will be collected at the end of the event.

What should I bring to a mapping event?

Please bring all the items in your TreesCount! Gear Pack from the training event – you will need your measuring tape, Tree Species ID guide, safety vest, and volunteer badge and lanyard!

I want to map trees but can’t always attend a mapping event. Can I map trees independently?

Yes, after you have attended a training event and one additional mapping event you can become an independent mapper. You can reserve any available block edges for mapping on your own. NYC Parks and TreesCount! partner groups will be lending out measuring wheels to independent mappers at Loaning Hubs. Once you have your block reserved and your measuring wheel borrowed, you’ll be able to head off on your own street tree mapping adventure! Get started reserving your block edges here – they will be reserved for you for seven days before being released back into the pool for another voluntreer to claim.

For additional information on NYC Parks, TreesCount! 2015 and past TreesCount! Censuses from 1995 and 2005 visit the NYC Parks website.
